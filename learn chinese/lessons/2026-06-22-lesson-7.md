---

**Taiwan vocabulary notes:**

- **認識** — literally "to recognize / to know." When meeting someone new: **很高興認識你** (nice to meet you). More natural in Taiwan than 幸會幸會 (too formal/old-fashioned).
- **保持聯絡** — the standard "keep in touch." Say it at the end of any new meeting. Stick it in muscle memory.
- **名片** — business cards still matter at Taiwan networking events and in professional settings. Even if you only have a phone, you may be asked.
- **轉職 vs 換跑道** — 轉職 (zhuǎnzhí) is neutral: changing jobs within a similar field. 換跑道 (huàn pǎodào) means a bigger pivot — changing career direction. In your case, you're doing both. 換跑道 is more casual, colloquial Taiwan.
- **打聽** — quietly asking around to learn what's going on. Very natural. 我想打聽一下 = "I wanted to ask around a bit." Slightly informal but useful.
- **加 LINE** — in Taiwan, LINE is the primary contact exchange. "Can I add you on LINE?" is the social equivalent of exchanging numbers. If someone says 加個 LINE, they like you.
- **業界** — "the industry." 行銷業界 (marketing industry), 科技業界 (tech industry). Very useful for talking about your professional world.

Radical: 耳 (ear)  
Memory hook: 👂 The ear (耳) on the left side — *listening* is how you stay *connected*.

Used in: 換跑道 (career pivot), 換工作 (change jobs), 換匯 (exchange currency)  
Radical: 手/扌(hand)

## ⟲ BLOCK 4 — Grammar Pattern (10 min)

### Pattern: 有機會的話 / 如果有機會
**Meaning: "If there's a chance…" / "When you get a chance…"**

This is one of the most naturally used soft-conditional phrases in Taiwan Mandarin. It softens requests, floats ideas, and opens doors — without sounding demanding.

---

**Structure:**

> 有機會的話，[suggestion or hope]。  
> *Yǒu jīhuì dehuà, [...].*  
> "If there's a chance / If the opportunity comes up, [...]."

It's interchangeable with 如果有機會… but slightly more conversational. You'll hear it constantly in Taiwan.

---

**Examples:**

| Chinese | Pinyin | English |
|---|---|---|
| 有機會的話，我們再聊聊。 | Yǒu jīhuì dehuà, wǒmen zài liáoliao. | If there's a chance, let's talk more. |
| 有機會的話，可以幫我介紹一下嗎？ | Yǒu jīhuì dehuà, kěyǐ bāng wǒ jièshào yíxià ma? | If you get a chance, could you introduce me? |
| 有機會的話，我想去那家公司看看。 | Yǒu jīhuì dehuà, wǒ xiǎng qù nà jiā gōngsī kànkàn. | If there's a chance, I'd like to check out that company. |
| 如果有機會，我想多了解一下你們的職缺。 | Rúguǒ yǒu jīhuì, wǒ xiǎng duō liǎojiě yíxià nǐmen de zhíquē. | If there's a chance, I'd like to learn more about your job openings. |
| 有機會的話，一起吃個飯吧。 | Yǒu jīhuì dehuà, yìqǐ chī gè fàn ba. | If there's a chance, let's grab a meal together. |

---

**Bonus: Soft requests with 幫**

> 有機會的話，可以**幫我**留意一下嗎？  
> *Yǒu jīhuì dehuà, kěyǐ bāng wǒ liúyì yíxià ma?*  
> "If you get a chance, could you keep an eye out for me?"

幫我 (bāng wǒ) = "help me" / "do this for me." Paired with 有機會的話, it becomes a very polite, low-pressure ask. Extremely useful in networking.

## ⟲ BLOCK 5 — Speaking / Dialogue (10 min)

### Scene — Meeting a contact at a Taipei coworking space / café

**Role A = Clark. Role B = 台灣本地人，在科技業工作 (a local Taiwanese person working in tech)**

---

> **B:** 你好，請問你是第一次來這裡嗎？  
> *Nǐ hǎo, qǐngwèn nǐ shì dì yī cì lái zhèlǐ ma?*  
> Hi, is this your first time here?

> **A:** 對啊，我朋友推薦這個地方。你常來嗎？  
> *Duì a, wǒ péngyǒu tuījiàn zhège dìfāng. Nǐ cháng lái ma?*  
> Yeah, a friend recommended this place. Do you come here often?

> **B:** 幾乎每天。我是在這裡辦公的。你在做什麼工作？  
> *Jīhū měitiān. Wǒ shì zài zhèlǐ bàngōng de. Nǐ zài zuò shénme gōngzuò?*  
> Almost every day. I work from here. What kind of work do you do?

> **A:** 我是做行銷的，主要是產品文案和品牌策略。我之前在宏碁做了十年，現在正在轉職。  
> *Wǒ shì zuò xíngxiāo de, zhǔyào shì chǎnpǐn wénàn hé pínpái cèlüè. Wǒ zhīqián zài Hóngqí zuòle shí nián, xiànzài zhèngzài zhuǎnzhí.*  
> I'm in marketing — mainly product copywriting and brand strategy. I was at Acer for ten years, and I'm currently making a career move.

> **B:** 哇，十年耶。你想找什麼樣的工作？  
> *Wā, shí nián ye. Nǐ xiǎng zhǎo shénme yàng de gōngzuò?*  
> Wow, ten years. What kind of work are you looking for?

> **A:** 我希望找行銷或品牌策略相關的。有機會的話，你有沒有認識業界的人可以介紹？  
> *Wǒ xīwàng zhǎo xíngxiāo huò pínpái cèlüè xiāngguān de. Yǒu jīhuì dehuà, nǐ yǒu méiyǒu rènshí yèjiè de rén kěyǐ jièshào?*  
> I'm hoping to find something in marketing or brand strategy. If there's a chance, do you know anyone in the industry you could introduce me to?

> **B:** 我在科技業，認識幾個做行銷的朋友。可以幫你打聽看看。  
> *Wǒ zài kējì yè, rènshí jǐ gè zuò xíngxiāo de péngyǒu. Kěyǐ bāng nǐ dǎtīng kànkàn.*  
> I'm in tech, but I know a few friends in marketing. I can ask around for you.

> **A:** 真的嗎？太感謝了。我們保持聯絡吧，可以加你的 LINE 嗎？  
> *Zhēn de ma? Tài gǎnxiè le. Wǒmen bǎochí liánluò ba, kěyǐ jiā nǐ de LINE ma?*  
> Really? Thank you so much. Let's stay in touch — can I add you on LINE?

> **B:** 當然可以。  
> *Dāngrán kěyǐ.*  
> Of course.

---

**Vocabulary from this dialogue:**

| Word | Pinyin | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| 幾乎 | jīhū | almost / nearly |
| 辦公 | bàngōng | to work / do office work |
| 相關 | xiāngguān | related / relevant |
| 當然 | dāngrán | of course |
| 太感謝了 | tài gǎnxiè le | thank you so much |
| 打聽看看 | dǎtīng kànkàn | ask around and see |

---

**Pronunciation notes:**

- **幾乎** jīhū — 1st + 1st, both flat. "Almost." Clean and useful for daily speech.
- **當然可以** dāngrán kěyǐ — 1st+2nd+3rd+3rd. The natural way to say "of course, sure." Learn it as one chunk.
- **太感謝了** tài gǎnxiè le — 4th+3rd+4th. "Thank you so much." Much warmer than just 謝謝.
- **打聽看看** — the 看看 (kànkàn) reduplication softens the action: "ask around and see / give it a try." Very common in Taiwan speech.
- Watch your tones on: 轉職 (zhuǎn 3rd zhí 2nd), 業界 (yè 4th jiè 4th)

---

**Follow-up — say these aloud from memory:**

1. 你好，很高興認識你。*(Nice to meet you.)*
2. 我是做行銷的，現在在轉職。*(I'm in marketing, currently changing jobs.)*
3. 有機會的話，可以幫我打聽一下嗎？*(If there's a chance, could you ask around for me?)*
4. 我們保持聯絡，可以加你的 LINE 嗎？*(Let's stay in touch — can I add you on LINE?)*
