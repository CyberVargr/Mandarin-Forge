---

**Taiwan vocabulary notes:**

- **離職原因** (lízhí yuányīn) — "reason for leaving." This question *will* come up. In Taiwan, interviewers ask it matter-of-factly. The safest and most honest answer for Clark: 我希望在新的環境找到不同的成長機會。(I'm hoping to find different growth opportunities in a new environment.) Never badmouth the last employer — just reframe it as growth.

- **期望薪資** (qīwàng xīnzī) — "expected salary." Often written on job applications in Taiwan. You can deflect with: 我想先了解整體待遇，再做討論。(I'd like to understand the full package first, then discuss.) More on this in the dialogue.

- **職涯目標** (zhíyā mùbiāo) — "career goal." 職涯 (career journey) vs. 職業 (occupation) — 職涯 is slightly more thoughtful and long-term. Use 職涯 in interviews.

- **挑戰性** (tiǎozhàn xìng) — "challenging / demanding" (as adjective). 有挑戰性的工作 = "a challenging job." Better than saying work is hard — it frames difficulty as something you seek out.

- **優點 vs. 缺點** — classic pair. Most interviewers expect you to turn your 缺點 into a disguised strength. Example: 我的缺點是有時候太在意細節，但這讓我的產出品質很穩定。(My weakness is that I sometimes care too much about details — but this keeps the quality of my output very consistent.)

- **主動** (zhǔdòng) — "proactive." 主動溝通 (proactive communication), 主動提案 (proactively proposing ideas). This word signals independence and leadership in Taiwan workplace culture.

- **壓力** (yālì) — "pressure / stress." 在高壓環境下 (under high-pressure conditions) + 也能 (can still) is a useful frame for interviews: "Even under pressure, I can..."

---

**2. 優 yōu — excellent / superior / advantage**  
Used in: 優點 (strength/advantage), 優秀 (excellent), 優先 (priority), 優惠 (discount/preferential)  
Radical: 人/亻 (person, left side)  
Components: 亻(person) + 憂 (right — worry/sorrow) *[Note: this is a historical composite — the 優 performer who played worry/comedy both]*  
Memory hook: 🎭 The person (亻) who plays all roles — excellence comes from range.  
Strokes: 17 | Tone: 1st (flat)

---

**3. 挑 tiǎo / tiāo — to challenge / to pick / to carry on a shoulder pole**  
Used in: 挑戰 (challenge), 挑剔 (picky), 挑選 (to select/choose)  
Radical: 扌 (hand, left side)  
Components: 扌(hand) + 兆 (omen/sign, right)  
Memory hook: ✋ A hand reaching toward an omen — 挑 is choosing to face something bigger than yourself.  
Strokes: 9 | Tone: 3rd in 挑戰 (tiǎo), 1st when meaning "to carry"

---

**4. 薪 xīn — salary / firewood**  
Used in: 薪水 (salary, lit. "salary water"), 薪資 (salary/remuneration), 加薪 (pay raise)  
Radical: 艸/草 (grass/plant, top)  
Components: 艹 (plant top) + 新 (new)  
Memory hook: 🌿 New plants (草+新) — in ancient times, bundles of grass and wood (薪) were your pay. Now it means the money you earn.  
Strokes: 16 | Tone: 1st (flat)  
*Taiwan usage: 期望薪資 is the standard phrase on job applications. 薪水 is more casual.*

---

**5. 壓 yā — to press / pressure**  
Used in: 壓力 (pressure/stress), 壓縮 (to compress), 壓低 (to push down), 低壓 (low pressure)  
Radical: 土 (earth, bottom)  
Components: 厭 (top — dislike/satiety) + 土 (earth, bottom)  
Memory hook: 🪨 Earth pressing down from above — that's 壓. Weight from all sides.  
Strokes: 17 | Tone: 1st (flat)  
*Note: 壓力 (pressure/stress) is one of the most useful compound words in adult Chinese. Know it cold.*

## ⟲ BLOCK 4 — Grammar Pattern (10 min)

### Pattern: 我離開 [X] 的原因是… / 我選擇 [X] 是因為…
**Meaning: Explaining decisions and reasons in a structured, interview-ready way**

In interviews, you're constantly asked *why* — why leaving, why applying here, why this direction. This pattern lets you answer calmly and clearly. It's more structured than just 因為 (because) alone — it frames the answer as a deliberate decision, not a reaction.

---

**Two forms:**

**Form A — Explaining a departure:**
> 我離開 [place/company/role]，是因為 [reason].  
> *Wǒ líkāi [X], shì yīnwèi [reason].*  
> "The reason I'm leaving [X] is because [reason]."

**Form B — Explaining a choice:**
> 我選擇 [X] 是因為 [reason].  
> *Wǒ xuǎnzé [X] shì yīnwèi [reason].*  
> "I'm choosing [X] because [reason]."

---

**Variant — using 原因是:**
> 我 [decision] 的原因是 [reason].  
> *Wǒ [decision] de yuányīn shì [reason].*  
> "The reason I [did X] is [reason]."

This sounds slightly more formal — good for written responses or more senior interviews.

---

**Examples:**

|---|---|---|
| 我離開宏碁，是因為想在新的環境尋找不同的成長機會。 | Wǒ líkāi Hóngqí, shì yīnwèi xiǎng zài xīn de huánjìng xúnzhǎo bùtóng de chéngzhǎng jīhuì. | I'm leaving Acer because I want to find different growth opportunities in a new environment. |

## ⟲ BLOCK 5 — Speaking / Dialogue (10 min)

### Scene — Mock job interview at a Taiwan tech/marketing company

**Role A = Clark (candidate). Role B = 面試官 (interviewer)**

This dialogue covers the four most common Taiwan interview questions after the self-intro:  
① Leaving reason → ② Strengths → ③ Weakness → ④ Salary

---

> **B:** 好的，謝謝您的自我介紹。請問您為什麼想離開現在的公司？  
> *Hǎo de, xièxie nín de zìwǒ jièshào. Qǐngwèn nín wèishénme xiǎng líkāi xiànzài de gōngsī?*  
> Thank you for your self-introduction. May I ask why you want to leave your current company?

> **A:** 在宏碁的十年讓我建立了非常扎實的基礎，我很感謝這段經歷。不過我覺得是時候去一個新的環境，把這些經驗用在更有挑戰性的方向上。  
> *Zài Hóngqí de shí nián ràng wǒ jiànlì le fēicháng zhāshí de jīchǔ, wǒ hěn gǎnxiè zhè duàn jīnglì. Búguò wǒ juéde shì shíhòu qù yīgè xīn de huánjìng, bǎ zhèxiē jīngyàn yòng zài gèng yǒu tiǎozhàn xìng de fāngxiàng shàng.*  
> Ten years at Acer gave me a very solid foundation, and I'm very grateful for that experience. But I feel it's time to move to a new environment and apply everything I've learned in a more challenging direction.

---

> **B:** 您認為您最大的優點是什麼？  
> *Nín rènwéi nín zuìdà de yōudiǎn shì shénme?*  
> What do you consider your greatest strength?

> **A:** 我最大的優點是能把複雜的技術或產品，用清楚有說服力的語言表達出來。我擅長從使用者的角度出發，把技術說得讓人聽得懂又記得住。在宏碁這十年，這個能力讓我參與了很多大型的產品發布。  
> *Wǒ zuìdà de yōudiǎn shì néng bǎ fùzá de jìshù huò chǎnpǐn, yòng qīngchǔ yǒu shuōfúlì de yǔyán biǎodá chūlái. Wǒ shàncháng cóng shǐyòng zhě de jiǎodù chūfā, bǎ jìshù shuō de ràng rén tīng de dǒng yòu jì de zhù. Zài Hóngqí zhè shí nián, zhège nénglì ràng wǒ cānyù le hěn duō dàxíng de chǎnpǐn fābù.*  
> My greatest strength is being able to express complex technology or products in language that's clear and persuasive. I'm good at starting from the user's perspective and explaining tech in a way people can understand and remember. Over ten years at Acer, this ability let me be part of many major product launches.

---

> **B:** 那您的缺點呢？  
> *Nà nín de quēdiǎn ne?*  
> And what about your weakness?

> **A:** 我有時候對細節太要求，可能會花比預期更多的時間去完善一份文件。不過我這幾年有意識地在調整，學會判斷什麼時候「夠好了」，什麼時候才需要繼續打磨。  
> *Wǒ yǒushíhòu duì xìjié tài yāoqiú, kěnéng huì huā bǐ yùqī gèng duō de shíjiān qù wánshàn yī fèn wénjian. Búguò wǒ zhè jǐ nián yǒuyìshí de zài tiáozhěng, xuéhuì pànduàn shénme shíhòu "gòu hǎo le", shénme shíhòu cái xūyào jìxù dǎmó.*  
> Sometimes I'm too focused on details — I might spend more time than expected perfecting a document. But over the past few years I've been consciously adjusting, learning to judge when something is "good enough" and when it really needs more refinement.

---

> **B:** 請問您的期望薪資是？  
> *Qǐngwèn nín de qīwàng xīnzī shì?*  
> What is your expected salary?

> **A:** 我想先了解一下這個職位的整體待遇結構，包括底薪和其他福利，然後再討論。如果範圍合理的話，我相信我們可以達成共識。  
> *Wǒ xiǎng xiān liǎojiě yīxià zhège zhíwèi de zhěngtǐ dàiyù jiégòu, bāokuò dǐxīn hé qítā fúlì, rán hòu zài tǎolùn. Rúguǒ fànwéi hélǐ de huà, wǒ xiāngxìn wǒmen kěyǐ dáchéng gòngshí.*  
> I'd like to first understand the overall compensation structure for this role — including base salary and other benefits — before discussing specifics. If the range is reasonable, I'm confident we can reach an agreement.

---

> **B:** 好的，最後，請問您有什麼想問我們的嗎？  
> *Hǎo de, zuìhòu, qǐngwèn nín yǒu shénme xiǎng wèn wǒmen de ma?*  
> Alright, finally — do you have any questions for us?

> **A:** 有的。我想了解一下，這個職位在未來一到兩年內，有什麼樣的成長空間？另外，您覺得一個新加入的人，最快能在哪裡貢獻到公司？  
> *Yǒu de. Wǒ xiǎng liǎojiě yīxià, zhège zhíwèi zài wèilái yī dào liǎng nián nèi, yǒu shénme yàng de chéngzhǎng kōngjiān? Lìngwài, nín juéde yīgè xīn jiārù de rén, zuì kuài néng zài nǎlǐ gòngxiàn dào gōngsī?*  
> Yes. I'd like to understand what the growth opportunities look like for this role over the next one to two years. Also, where do you think a new person coming in could contribute to the company most quickly?

**Pronunciation notes:**

- **扎實** (zhāshí) — 1st+2nd. The 扎 uses the zh sound (retroflexed). Common compliment in Taiwan: "You've built a very 扎實 foundation."
- **是時候** (shì shíhòu) — 4th+2nd+4th. Natural and speakable. 是時候了 (it's time now) is even more common in casual speech.
- **有意識地** (yǒuyìshí de) — hard to say fast. Slow it down: yǒu — yì — shí — de. The 意識 part means "awareness / consciousness." 有意識地調整 = "consciously adjusting."
- **打磨** (dǎmó) — 3rd+2nd. Beautiful compound for a copywriter: 打磨文案 = "polishing the copy." Sounds very professional and craft-oriented.
- **達成共識** (dáchéng gòngshí) — 2nd+2nd+4th+2nd. Useful closer for salary discussions. Signals you're collaborative, not transactional.

### Why I'm leaving — three options

**Option A (Growth-forward):**  
我在宏碁建立了非常紮實的基礎，但我覺得是時候去更有挑戰性的環境了。  
*Wǒ zài Hóngqí jiànlì le fēicháng zhāshí de jīchǔ, dàn wǒ juéde shì shíhòu qù gèng yǒu tiǎozhàn xìng de huánjìng le.*  
I built a very solid foundation at Acer, but I feel it's time to move to a more challenging environment.

**Option B (Skill-application framing):**  
我希望把這十年學到的東西，帶到一個能真正發揮的地方。  
*Wǒ xīwàng bǎ zhè shí nián xué dào de dōngxi, dài dào yīgè néng zhēnzhèng fāhuī de dìfāng.*  
I want to take what I've learned over ten years and bring it somewhere where I can really put it to use.

**Option C (Honest and Taiwan-appropriate):**  
在一家公司待了十年，是很難得的經驗。現在我想用這些經驗，去嘗試不同的挑戰。  
*Zài yījiā gōngsī dài le shí nián, shì hěn nándé de jīngyàn. Xiànzài wǒ xiǎng yòng zhèxiē jīngyàn, qù cháng shì bùtóng de tiǎozhàn.*  
Staying at one company for ten years is a rare experience. Now I want to use that experience to try different challenges.

### Strengths — in your own voice

我做事比較主動，不需要別人一直催。  
*Wǒ zuòshì bǐjiào zhǔdòng, bù xūyào biérén yīzhí cuī.*

### Salary — deflect professionally

我目前的優先順序是找到跟我的專長和職涯目標符合的機會。待遇的部分，我相信只要方向對了，我們一定能討論出一個合理的結果。  
*Wǒ mùqián de yōuxiān shùnxù shì zhǎodào gēn wǒ de zhuānchǎng hé zhíyā mùbiāo fúhé de jīhuì. Dàiyù de bùfen, wǒ xiāngxìn zhǐyào fāngxiàng duì le, wǒmen yīdìng néng tǎolùn chū yīgè hélǐ de jiéguǒ.*  
My current priority is finding an opportunity that fits my expertise and career goals. As for compensation — I believe as long as the direction is right, we can definitely discuss and reach a reasonable outcome.

### Q&A question to ask at the end of any interview

除了日常的工作任務以外，您覺得這個職位最重要的貢獻是什麼？  
*Chúle rìcháng de gōngzuò rènwù yǐwài, nín juéde zhège zhíwèi zuì zhòngyào de gòngxiàn shì shénme?*  
Beyond the day-to-day tasks, what do you think the most important contribution of this role is?

*(This question signals strategic thinking — you're not just asking about the job description, you're thinking about impact.)*
