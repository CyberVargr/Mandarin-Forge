## 2. New Vocabulary — 10 min

**Topic: MRT + convenience store + food on the go (Taiwan)**

| 中文 | Pinyin | English | Example sentence |
|---|---|---|---|
| 捷運 | jiéyùn | MRT (metro) | 我坐捷運去上班。(I take the MRT to work.) |
| 悠遊卡 | yōuyóukǎ | EasyCard | 我的悠遊卡沒錢了。(My EasyCard is out of money.) |
| 超商 | chāoshāng | convenience store | 我去超商買早餐。(I go to the convenience store for breakfast.) |
| 便當 | biàndāng | bento / lunchbox | 我中午吃便當。(I eat a lunchbox at noon.) |
| 加熱 | jiārè | to heat up | 可以幫我加熱嗎？(Can you heat this up for me?) |
| 轉乘 | zhuǎnchéng | to transfer (lines) | 我要在台北車站轉乘。(I transfer at Taipei Main Station.) |
| 出口 | chūkǒu | exit | 我在二號出口等你。(I'll wait at Exit 2.) |
| 直走 | zhízǒu | go straight | 直走，然後右轉。(Go straight, then turn right.) |

**Taiwan vocab notes:**
- **超商** = 便利商店 (7-11, 全家…). In speech people say 超商 or just the brand name: 「我去全家」.
- **悠遊卡** — the card you tap for 捷運, buses, YouBike, and 超商 purchases. Essential word.
- **便當** is the Taiwan word (Mainland often says 盒饭). 雞腿便當 (chicken-leg bento) is the classic order.
- **加熱** — at any 超商 the staff will ask 「要加熱嗎？」 (Heat it up?). Answer: 要，謝謝 / 不用，謝謝.
- **轉乘** — the exact word announced and signposted in the 捷運 system. Worth owning.

## 3. Character Writing — 10 min

Write each character 5× by hand. Read the components and memory hook first.

---

**走 (zǒu) — to walk / to go**

A person striding — 土 (earth) on top of 龰 (a stylized foot at the bottom). The whole character *is* legs in motion.

Uses: 直走 (go straight), 走路 (walk), 走吧 (let's go), 慢走 (take care — said as someone leaves)

Memory hook: *Feet pounding the earth — walking.*

---

**口 (kǒu) — mouth / opening**

The simplest pictograph: a square open mouth. As a component it appears everywhere.

Uses: 出口 (exit), 入口 (entrance), 門口 (doorway), 人口 (population)

Memory hook: *A wide-open square mouth — an opening.*

---

**站 (zhàn) — station / to stand**

Components: 立 (lì = to stand) + 占 (zhàn = to occupy). You *stand and occupy* a spot — and it's where the train stops.

Uses: 捷運站 (MRT station), 車站 (train/bus station), 台北車站 (Taipei Main Station), 站起來 (stand up)

Memory hook: *Stand and hold your spot — that's a station.*

---

**車 (chē) — vehicle / car**

A pictograph of a cart seen from above: the 田 is the cart body and axle, the vertical line is the axle pole. Ancient and everywhere.

Uses: 車站 (station), 火車 (train), 機車 (scooter —核心 Taiwan word), 上車/下車 (get on/off)

Memory hook: *A cart and its axle, drawn from above.*

## 4. Grammar Pattern — 10 min

### 怎麼 (zěnme) + verb — "how to…"

The go-to pattern for directions and figuring things out. 怎麼 sits **before** the verb.

**Structure:** [Place/Thing] + 怎麼 + [verb]？

| Chinese | Pinyin | English |
|---|---|---|
| 捷運站怎麼走？| Jiéyùn zhàn zěnme zǒu? | How do I get to the MRT station? |
| 這個怎麼用？| Zhège zěnme yòng? | How do I use this? |
| 台北車站怎麼去？| Táiběi chēzhàn zěnme qù? | How do I get to Taipei Main Station? |
| 這個字怎麼念？| Zhège zì zěnme niàn? | How do you pronounce this character? |
| 悠遊卡怎麼加值？| Yōuyóukǎ zěnme jiāzhí? | How do I top up the EasyCard? |

**Don't confuse:**
- 怎麼 (zěnme) = *how* → 怎麼走？
- 怎麼樣 (zěnmeyàng) = *how about / how is it?* → 你覺得怎麼樣？(from Lesson 3)

**Upgrade — stack with 覺得 + 之後:**

- 我覺得坐捷運比較快。
  *Wǒ juéde zuò jiéyùn bǐjiào kuài.*
  "I think taking the MRT is faster."
- 下班之後，我坐捷運回家。
  *Xiàbān zhīhòu, wǒ zuò jiéyùn huí jiā.*
  "After work, I take the MRT home."

## 5. Dialogue — 10 min

**Two short, real scenes.** Role A = 你 (Clark).

---

### Scene 1 — 超商 (convenience store)

> **A:** 你好，我要這個便當。
> *Nǐ hǎo, wǒ yào zhège biàndāng.*
> Hi, I'll take this bento.

> **B (店員):** 好的，要加熱嗎？
> *Hǎo de, yào jiārè ma?*
> Sure, want it heated up?

> **A:** 要，謝謝。可以用悠遊卡嗎？
> *Yào, xièxie. Kěyǐ yòng yōuyóukǎ ma?*
> Yes, thanks. Can I pay with EasyCard?

> **B:** 可以。嗶一下就好。
> *Kěyǐ. Bì yíxià jiù hǎo.*
> Sure. Just tap it.

---

### Scene 2 — asking directions in the 捷運

> **A:** 不好意思，請問台北101怎麼去？
> *Bùhǎoyìsi, qǐngwèn Táiběi yāo-líng-yāo zěnme qù?*
> Excuse me, how do I get to Taipei 101?

> **B:** 你要在這裡轉乘，坐到101站，從四號出口出去。
> *Nǐ yào zài zhèlǐ zhuǎnchéng, zuò dào yāo-líng-yāo zhàn, cóng sì hào chūkǒu chūqù.*
> You transfer here, ride to the 101 stop, and leave from Exit 4.

> **A:** 好，謝謝！那個出口直走就到了嗎？
> *Hǎo, xièxie! Nàge chūkǒu zhízǒu jiù dào le ma?*
> Got it, thanks! From that exit, do I just go straight and I'm there?

> **B:** 對，直走大概五分鐘。
> *Duì, zhízǒu dàgài wǔ fēnzhōng.*
> Yes, about five minutes straight ahead.

---

**Pronunciation notes:**
- **嗶一下** (bì yíxià) = "give it a beep/tap." Onomatopoeia for tapping the card — pure Taiwan daily speech.
- **101** is read **yāo-líng-yāo**, not yī-líng-yī. 一 becomes 幺 (yāo) when reading out digits aloud.
- **不好意思** (bùhǎoyìsi) — your all-purpose "excuse me / sorry to bother you." Use it before every question to a stranger.
- **就好** (jiù hǎo) = "and that's it / that's all you need." Very common closer.

**Follow-up questions (answer aloud):**

1. 你常去哪一家超商？7-11、全家、還是別的？
2. 你平常怎麼去上班？坐捷運、騎機車、還是走路？

## 6. Self-Expression + Mini Quiz — 10 min

### Today's self-expression sentences

Real things you can say about your actual Taipei life right now.

---

**1. 我平常坐捷運上下班，很方便。**
*Wǒ píngcháng zuò jiéyùn shàng-xià bān, hěn fāngbiàn.*
"I usually take the MRT to and from work — it's very convenient."

- Casual: 我都坐捷運啦，很方便。
- Polished: 捷運是我每天通勤的主要方式。("The MRT is my main daily commute method.")

---

**2. 中午我通常在超商買便當，比較快。**
*Zhōngwǔ wǒ tōngcháng zài chāoshāng mǎi biàndāng, bǐjiào kuài.*
"At noon I usually grab a bento at the convenience store — it's faster."

- Note: 比較快 (bǐjiào kuài) = "comparatively faster." 比較 softens any comparison naturally.

---

**3. 我覺得台北的交通很方便。**
*Wǒ juéde Táiběi de jiāotōng hěn fāngbiàn.*
"I think Taipei's transportation is very convenient."

- Pattern callback: 我覺得…很… (from Lesson 3), now applied to city life.
- 交通 (jiāotōng) = transportation/traffic — useful daily word.
