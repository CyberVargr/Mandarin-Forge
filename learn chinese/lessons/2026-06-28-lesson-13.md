---

**Vocabulary notes:**

- **轉換跑道** (zhuǎnhuàn pǎodào) — literally "switch running lanes." The Taiwan metaphor for a career pivot. Very natural. 跑道 = track / runway. Common in conversations about job changes. Compare with 轉職 (zhuǎnzhí) from L7 — same concept, but 轉換跑道 implies a bigger shift, not just a different company.

- **充電** (chōngdiàn) — literally "to charge a battery." In Taiwan, used constantly for "taking time to rest and recover your energy." 充電一下 = recharge a bit. When someone leaves a stressful job, 先充電一下 is the natural way to say "take some time first." You'll hear this and want to say it.

- **自在** (zìzài) — first+fourth. "At ease / feeling free and comfortable in a given space or situation." Different from 輕鬆 (relaxed) — 自在 is about being yourself without performance. A person who walks into a room and belongs there is 自在 people. A workspace where you don't have to pretend is 讓人感到自在.

- **踏實** (tāshí) — first+second. "Grounded / solid." When someone works without chasing shortcuts or big flashy results, that person is 踏實. 一步一步、踏踏實實地做 = step by step, solidly, without drama. Clark energy.

- **斜槓** (xiégàng) — literally a "slash" character /. Refers to "slash careers" — people who do multiple things: 行銷人 / 音樂人 / 創作者. Taiwan media uses this constantly. If you freelance + have creative projects + look for full-time work simultaneously, you're a 斜槓青年 (slash-career young person).

- **嘗試** (chángshì) — third+fourth. "To try / attempt something new." 我想嘗試一下 = I want to give it a try. Warmer and more intentional than 試試看 (try and see what happens). Use it when describing something deliberate you're doing.

## ⟲ BLOCK 3 — Character Writing (10 min)

Write each character 5× by hand.

---

**1. 轉 zhuǎn — to turn / transition / transfer / switch**  
Used in: 轉換跑道 (career pivot), 轉職 (job change), 轉移 (transfer), 轉變 (change/transformation)  
Radical: 車 (vehicle/wheel, left side)  
Components: 車 (vehicle) + 專 (focused/specialized, right)  
Memory hook: 🚗 A wheel (車) spinning with focused force (專) — that's 轉. A career transition is a wheel turning in a new direction.  
Strokes: 18 | Tone: 3rd (zhuǎn, dipping)  
*Key compounds to write: 轉換、轉職、轉變*

*(High stroke count — take it slow. The 車 radical on the left is compressed. The right 專 has its own internal structure: 丿+ 聿 + 寸. Trace in a slow line first.)*

---

**2. 創 chuàng — to create / found / initiate**  
Used in: 創作 (creative work), 創業 (start a business), 創意 (creativity — from L3!), 創新 (innovation)  
Radical: 刀/刂 (knife, right side)  
Components: 倉 (warehouse/storehouse) + 刂 (knife, right)  
Memory hook: 🗡️ A knife (刂) cutting open a storehouse (倉) — releasing what's inside. That's 創. Creation is opening something that was stored. Your creative projects are 創作.  
Strokes: 12 | Tone: 4th (chuàng, falling)  
*Key compounds to write: 創作、創意、創業、創新*

*(Note: 創意 appeared in Lesson 3. 創 is the same character — you've seen it before. Now you can write it.)*

---

**3. 充 chōng — to fill / charge / supplement**  
Used in: 充電 (recharge), 補充 (supplement — from L8!), 充足 (sufficient), 充實 (enrich/fulfill)  
Radical: 儿 (legs, at the bottom)  
Components: 亠 (lid, top) + 厶 (private/small, middle) + 儿 (legs, bottom)  
Memory hook: 🔋 Something (亠 lid) pressing energy down into legs (儿) that are ready to go. 充 is filling up — a battery, a person, a day.  
Strokes: 6 | Tone: 1st (chōng, flat)  
*Key compounds to write: 充電、補充、充足、充實*

*(6 strokes — one of the simpler characters this week. But it carries a lot of vocabulary. 充實 means "enriching / fulfilling" — 生活很充實 = life feels full and meaningful.)*

---

**4. 踏 tā / tà — to step / to tread**  
Used in: 踏實 (grounded), 踏入 (step into), 腳踏實地 (keep both feet on the ground)  
Radical: 足 (foot, left side)  
Components: 足 (foot) + 沓 (layers/repeated, right)  
Memory hook: 👣 A foot (足) pressing down layer by layer (沓). 踏 is the act of deliberate stepping — each step placed on purpose. 踏實 = each step is real and solid.  
Strokes: 15 | Tone: 4th (tà, falling)  
*Key compounds to write: 踏實、腳踏實地、踏入*

*(Note: 腳踏實地 (jiǎo tā shí dì) is a four-character idiom — "feet planted firmly on the ground." It means being down-to-earth and practical. Good to recognize.)*

---

**5. 嘗 cháng — to taste / to try / to experience**  
Used in: 嘗試 (to attempt/try), 嘗到 (to taste/experience — success, bitterness, etc.)  
Radical: 口 (mouth, at the bottom)  
Components: 尚 (still/yet, upper area) + 旨 (purpose/delicious, middle) + 口 (mouth, bottom)  
Memory hook: 👅 A mouth (口) that has purpose (旨) and still keeps tasting (尚). 嘗 is intentional tasting — you open your mouth because you want to know what something is like. That's what 嘗試 means: "I'm opening myself up to find out."  
Strokes: 14 | Tone: 2nd (cháng, rising)  
*Key compounds to write: 嘗試、嘗到成功的滋味*

*(嘗到成功的滋味 — "to taste the flavor of success." Nice phrase to know.)*

## ⟲ BLOCK 4 — Grammar Pattern (10 min)

### Pattern: 打算… (dǎsuàn…) — "planning to / intending to"

This pattern is one of the most useful you'll use in the next few months. Every person you meet who knows you left Acer will ask: **你接下來打算做什麼？** (What are you planning to do next?) You need a clean, confident answer in Chinese. 打算 gives you that structure.

---

**Structure:**

> 我打算 + [verb phrase]  
> *Wǒ dǎsuàn + …*  
> "I'm planning to / I intend to…"

**Negative:**  
> 我沒有打算 + [verb phrase] — "I'm not planning to…"

**Question:**  
> 你打算怎麼做？ — "What are you planning to do?"  
> 你接下來打算做什麼？ — "What are you planning to do next?"

---

**Five examples — read aloud:**

1. 我打算先充電一下，再認真找工作。  
   *Wǒ dǎsuàn xiān chōngdiàn yīxià, zài rènzhēn zhǎo gōngzuò.*  
   I'm planning to recharge first, then seriously look for work.

2. 我打算嘗試自由接案，邊找正職邊累積案子。  
   *Wǒ dǎsuàn chángshì zìyóu jiē àn, biān zhǎo zhèngzhí biān lèijī àn zi.*  
   I'm planning to try freelancing — building up projects while looking for full-time work.

3. 我打算繼續待在台灣，這裡是我的家。  
   *Wǒ dǎsuàn jìxù dāi zài Táiwān, zhèlǐ shì wǒ de jiā.*  
   I'm planning to stay in Taiwan — it's my home.

4. 我打算在行銷或品牌策略的方向繼續發展。  
   *Wǒ dǎsuàn zài xíngxiāo huò pǐnpái cèlüè de fāngxiàng jìxù fāzhǎn.*  
   I'm planning to keep developing in marketing or brand strategy.

5. 我沒有打算馬上接受第一個機會——我想找真的合適的。  
   *Wǒ méiyǒu dǎsuàn mǎshàng jiēshòu dì yī gè jīhuì — wǒ xiǎng zhǎo zhēn de héshì de.*  
   I'm not planning to take the first opportunity that comes — I want to find something that's actually a good fit.

---

**Compare: 打算 vs. 想**

> 我想充電一下。— "I want to recharge." (desire, feeling)  
> 我打算先充電。— "I'm planning to recharge first." (intention, decision)  

打算 sounds more settled and thought-out. Use it when describing deliberate choices. Use 想 for softer wishes or things you haven't decided yet.

---

**Compare: 打算 vs. 計畫**

> 我打算找新工作。— "I'm planning to find a new job." (natural spoken)  
> 我有計畫要找新工作。— "I have a plan to find a new job." (slightly more structured)  

打算 flows better in casual conversation. 計畫 sounds slightly more formal or pre-organized.

**Vocabulary from this dialogue:**

| Word | Pinyin | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| 好久不見 | hǎojiǔ bùjiàn | long time no see |
| 轉換跑道 | zhuǎnhuàn pǎodào | career pivot |
| 心情還可以 | xīnqíng hái kěyǐ | feeling okay / not bad emotionally |
| 正式離職 | zhèngshì lízhí | officially leaving a job |
| 是時候 | shì shíhòu | it's time (to) |
| 接下來 | jiē xià lái | next / from here on |
| 同時 | tóngshí | at the same time / simultaneously |
| 案子 | àn zi | case / project (freelance) |
| 對我來說 | duì wǒ lái shuō | for me / as far as I'm concerned |
| 不只是 | bù zhǐshì | not only / not just |
| 說得很好 | shuō de hěn hǎo | well said |
| 一定要 | yīdìng yào | definitely / absolutely |

---

**Pronunciation notes:**

- **好久不見** (hǎojiǔ bùjiàn) — 3rd+3rd+4th+4th. The double 3rd tones at the start: hǎo lifts, jiǔ dips. Then bùjiàn falls twice. Very common greeting — own it.

- **是時候** (shì shíhòu) — 4th+2nd+4th. "It's time to." Shí (second tone) rises in the middle. This phrase hits naturally in conversation when you're explaining a decision.

- **對我來說** (duì wǒ lái shuō) — 4th+3rd+2nd+1st. A powerful phrase. "For me / in my experience / from my perspective." Use it constantly when expressing personal views or situation. More natural than 對我而言 in spoken Taiwan Mandarin.

- **接下來** (jiē xià lái) — 1st+4th+2nd. "Next / from here on / going forward." Essential for any conversation about plans, career transition, or the next chapter. Learn the rhythm: JIĒ-xià-lái.

- **一定要** (yīdìng yào) — 1st+4th+4th. Three falling-direction syllables, the first one flat. "Definitely / absolutely." Used for enthusiastic confirmation. Add it to 好啊 to make an agreement feel warm and genuine: 好啊，一定要！

---

**Read-aloud practice:**  
Read Role A's lines straight through. Focus on:  
1. The rhythm of 是時候換個方向了 — say it as one thought, not word by word  
2. The tones in 對我來說已經是家了 — all four tones present in one sentence  
3. 好啊，一定要！— make it sound enthusiastic, not flat

*Jiànshēn ràng wǒ xué dào yī jiàn shì: jìnbù bú shì kào línggǎn, ér shì kào měitiān de xíguàn. Zhōngwén yě shì yīyàng.*  
Training taught me something: progress isn't about inspiration — it's about daily habits. Chinese is the same.

**5. On creative projects**

我有一些創作上的想法，還在醞釀中。未來某一天，我想把它們做出來。  
*Wǒ yǒu yīxiē chuàngzuò shàng de xiǎngfǎ, hái zài yùnniàng zhōng. Wèilái mǒu yī tiān, wǒ xiǎng bǎ tāmen zuò chūlái.*  
I have some creative ideas still brewing. Someday, I want to make them real.

*(Simple version:)*  
我有自己的創作計畫，慢慢來。

I have my own creative plans — no rush.

**6. On not having everything figured out**

我不需要現在就知道所有的答案。我只需要繼續前進，踏實地做。  
*Wǒ bù xūyào xiànzài jiù zhīdào suǒyǒu de dá'àn. Wǒ zhǐ xūyào jìxù qiánjìn, tāshí de zuò.*  
I don't need to know all the answers right now. I just need to keep moving forward and do things solidly.

**Vocabulary note — 醞釀 (yùnniàng):**  
Third+fourth. Literally "to ferment wine" — extended meaning: ideas or plans slowly taking shape, brewing without forcing. 還在醞釀中 = "still brewing / still taking form." Used for creative ideas, feelings, decisions that aren't ready yet. A beautiful and accurate word for where some of your projects probably live right now.
