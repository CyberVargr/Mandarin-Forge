**Taiwan vocabulary notes:**

- **早午餐** (zǎowǔcān) — "brunch." 早餐 (breakfast) + 午餐 (lunch) = 早午餐. Taiwan brunch culture is huge — especially in Taipei. Popular spots have waits. If you're somewhere on Da'an or Zhongshan on a Saturday morning, 排隊一下 is normal.

- **排隊** (páiduì) — "to line up / queue." Taiwan people queue very politely. Common at popular 早午餐, night market stalls, and 超商 ATMs. 要排隊 (have to queue) / 不用排隊 (no need to queue).

- **內用 / 外帶** (nèiyòng / wàidài) — "dine in / take away." You'll hear this at every café and 超商. Short question from staff: 內用還是外帶？ Short answer: 內用，謝謝 or 外帶好了.

- **附近** (fùjìn) — "nearby / in the area." 附近有沒有… (is there a nearby…) is one of the most useful location phrases in Taiwan daily life. More natural than 旁邊 (right next to) when asking about general area.

- **巷子** (xiàngzi) — "alley / lane." Taipei's best cafés and food spots are often in lanes off main roads. 在巷子裡 = in the alley. This word comes up constantly. Learn it once and it unlocks a lot of navigation talk.

- **悠閒** (yōuxián) — "leisurely / relaxed." First tone + second tone. Describes the energy of a good Saturday. 悠閒地喝咖啡 (leisurely drinking coffee). Taiwan people love this vibe and say this word often on weekends.

## ⟲ BLOCK 3 — Character Writing (10 min)

Write each character 5× by hand. Read the components first.

---

**1. 排 pái — to arrange / to line up / to queue**  
Used in: 排隊 (queue), 排列 (arrangement), 排名 (ranking)  
Radical: 扌 (hand, left side)  
Components: 扌(hand) + 非 (wrong/not, right)  
Memory hook: ✋ A hand (扌) pushing against something that says "no" (非) — forcing people into a line. That's 排. You queue because someone is managing the order.  
Strokes: 11 | Tone: 2nd (pái, rising)  
*Key compounds to write: 排隊、排列、排名*

---

**2. 推 tuī — to push / to recommend / to promote**  
Used in: 推薦 (recommend), 推進 (push forward — from Lesson 11!), 推廣 (promote/spread)  
Radical: 扌 (hand, left side)  
Components: 扌(hand) + 隹 (short-tailed bird, right)  
Memory hook: ✋ A hand pushing a bird forward. 推 is motion — pushing something out into the world. 推薦 = push someone toward a recommendation.  
Strokes: 11 | Tone: 1st (tuī, flat)  
*Key compounds to write: 推薦、推進、推廣*

*(Note: 推 appeared in Lesson 7 as 推薦 and Lesson 11 as 推進. Same character, two uses you already know.)*

---

**3. 閒 xián — leisure / free time / idle**  
Used in: 悠閒 (leisurely), 休閒 (recreation), 閒聊 (casual chat), 空閒 (free time)  
Radical: 門 (gate, outer frame)  
Components: 門 (gate) + 木 (tree, inside)  
Memory hook: 🌳 A tree growing inside a gate — nothing pressing, nothing urgent. Just a tree being a tree. That's 閒. Leisure is when there's space for a tree inside your gate.  
Strokes: 12 | Tone: 2nd (xián, rising)  
*Key compounds to write: 悠閒、休閒、閒聊*

---

**4. 附 fù — to attach / nearby / supplementary**  
Used in: 附近 (nearby), 附上 (attach / enclose), 附件 (attachment — work email term!)  
Radical: 阝 (mound/place, left side — the one on the left)  
Components: 阝(place) + 付 (to pay/hand over, right)  
Memory hook: 🗺️ A place (阝) where you hand something over (付) — 附 is things that cling or belong together. 附近 = the places that cling near you.  
Strokes: 8 | Tone: 4th (fù, falling)  
*Key compounds to write: 附近、附上、附件*

*(附件 (fùjiàn) = email attachment — this will come up in your job hunt. Know it now.)*

---

**5. 菜 cài — vegetable / dish / food item**  
Used in: 菜單 (menu), 點菜 (to order food), 菜色 (dishes available), 素菜 (vegetarian dish)  
Radical: 艹 (grass/plant, top)  
Components: 艹(plant) + 采 (to pick/pluck, below)  
Memory hook: 🥦 A plant (艹) being picked (采) — that's a vegetable. A 菜單 is literally a "vegetable list" — what's available to pick from.  
Strokes: 11 | Tone: 4th (cài, falling)  
*Key compounds to write: 菜單、點菜、素菜*

## ⟲ BLOCK 4 — Grammar Pattern (10 min)

### Pattern: 有沒有… (yǒu méiyǒu…) — "Is there a…? / Do you have a…?"

This is one of the most useful question patterns in Taiwan daily life. It's a yes/no question built by putting 有 (have/there is) and 沒有 (don't have/there isn't) side by side. It's softer and more natural than simply 有嗎? (do you have?) — and more direct than 請問…嗎? in casual situations.

---

**Structure:**

> 有沒有 + [noun / noun phrase] + (嗎)?  
> *yǒu méiyǒu + [X]?*  
> "Is there a [X]? / Do you have [X]?"

**Answers:**  
> 有 (yǒu) = Yes, there is / I have one  
> 沒有 (méiyǒu) = No, there isn't / I don't have one  

---

**Five examples — read aloud:**

1. 附近有沒有好喝的咖啡廳？  
   *Fùjìn yǒu méiyǒu hào hē de kāfēitīng?*  
   Is there a good café nearby?

2. 你有沒有時間今天一起吃早午餐？  
   *Nǐ yǒu méiyǒu shíjiān jīntiān yīqǐ chī zǎowǔcān?*  
   Do you have time to grab brunch together today?

3. 這家店有沒有素食的選擇？  
   *Zhè jiā diàn yǒu méiyǒu sùshí de xuǎnzé?*  
   Does this place have vegetarian options?

4. 你有沒有去過信義區那邊的夜市？  
   *Nǐ yǒu méiyǒu qù guò Xìnyì Qū nà biān de yèshì?*  
   Have you ever been to the night market in the Xinyi area?

5. 這個週末你有沒有什麼計畫？  
   *Zhège zhōumò nǐ yǒu méiyǒu shénme jìhuà?*  
   Do you have any plans this weekend?

---

**Compare: two ways to ask about nearby places**

> 附近有沒有…？ → more direct, casual, used when you just want a yes/no  
> 請問附近有…嗎？ → slightly more polite, good when asking a stranger or shop staff

Both work. Prefer 有沒有 in casual talk; add 請問 when addressing someone you don't know.

> There's a small market nearby, every weekend. About a five-minute walk.

**Vocabulary from this dialogue:**

| Word | Pinyin | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| 幾位 | jǐ wèi | how many people (polite) |
| 靠窗 | kào chuāng | by the window |
| 位子 | wèizi | seat / spot |
| 班尼迪克蛋 | bānnídíkè dàn | eggs Benedict |
| 受歡迎 | shòu huānyíng | popular / well-received |
| 點 | diǎn | to order (food) |
| 稍等一下 | shāo děng yīxià | wait just a moment (polite) |
| 逛 | guàng | to browse / wander around |
| 市集 | shìjí | market / bazaar |
| 不錯耶 | búcuò yē | nice / not bad (casual, very Taiwan) |

---

**Pronunciation notes:**

- **內用** (nèiyòng) — 4th+4th. Both falling. Short, clipped: NÈI-YÒNG. You'll say this dozens of times in Taiwan.

- **稍等一下** (shāo děng yīxià) — 1st+3rd+neutral+4th. The 稍 (slightly/just) makes it softer than 等一下 alone. Staff use it constantly. Learn to recognize it.

- **不錯耶** (búcuò yē) — the 耶 (yē) at the end is pure Taiwan flavor. It's an enthusiastic sentence-ending particle — somewhere between "nice!" and "wow!" Textbooks don't teach it. People use it constantly in casual speech.

- **逛** (guàng) — 4th tone, falling. "To browse / wander without a specific destination." 逛街 (wander the streets / go shopping), 逛市場 (browse a market). Different from 去 (go to a specific destination).

- **市集** (shìjí) — 4th+2nd. Weekend markets. Taipei has many: 建國假日花市, 光華商場一帶, seasonal pop-ups. 市集 is more artisan/craft-market; 夜市 is night market. Both useful.

## ⟲ BLOCK 6 — Self-Expression: Life in Taiwan, in Chinese (10 min)

### Saturday morning voice

These are sentences for real Saturday-morning thoughts. Not interview answers — real life.

---

**1. What a Saturday morning sounds like**

今天是假日，我不需要趕時間。  
*Jīntiān shì jiǎrì, wǒ bù xūyào gǎn shíjiān.*  
Today is a holiday, I don't need to rush.

*(More casual version:)*  
今天放假，慢慢來就好。  
*Jīntiān fàngjià, màn màn lái jiù hǎo.*  
Day off today — no rush.

---

**2. Talking about Taipei neighborhoods and cafés**

我很喜歡台北有那麼多的巷子咖啡廳，每一間都有自己的個性。  
*Wǒ hěn xǐhuān Táiběi yǒu nàme duō de xiàngzi kāfēitīng, měi yī jiān dōu yǒu zìjǐ de gèxìng.*  
I really like how Taipei has so many alley cafés — each one has its own character.

*(Simpler version:)*  
台北的咖啡廳很有特色，我很喜歡去逛。  
*Táiběi de kāfēitīng hěn yǒu tèsè, wǒ hěn xǐhuān qù guàng.*  
Taipei's cafés have great character — I love wandering around them.

---

**3. The transition ahead**

我下週就要離職了，但今天先休息一下。  
*Wǒ xià zhōu jiù yào lízhí le, dàn jīntiān xiān xiūxi yīxià.*  
I'm leaving my job next week, but today I'm resting first.

*(Casual version:)*  
下週要離職，今天先放鬆一下再說。  
*Xià zhōu yào lízhí, jīntiān xiān fàngsōng yīxià zài shuō.*  
Leaving next week — today I rest first, then deal with the rest.

---

**4. On being in Taiwan and learning Chinese**

我在台灣住了十幾年，但中文還在學。有些事情，語言學得慢，但感情學得快。  
*Wǒ zài Táiwān zhù le shí jǐ nián, dàn Zhōngwén hái zài xué. Yǒuxiē shìqíng, yǔyán xué de màn, dàn gǎnqíng xué de kuài.*  
I've lived in Taiwan for over ten years, but I'm still learning Chinese. Some things — you learn the language slowly, but the feeling fast.

*(Simpler version:)*  
我還在學中文，但台灣已經是我的家了。  
*Wǒ hái zài xué Zhōngwén, dàn Táiwān yǐjīng shì wǒ de jiā le.*  
I'm still learning Chinese, but Taiwan is already my home.

---

**5. Personal growth, quietly**

我不需要每天都有很大的突破，只要繼續前進就好。  
*Wǒ bù xūyào měitiān dōu yǒu hěn dà de túpò, zhǐyào jìxù qiánjìn jiù hǎo.*  
I don't need a big breakthrough every day — I just need to keep moving forward.

---

**Vocabulary from Block 6:**

| Word | Pinyin | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| 趕時間 | gǎn shíjiān | to be in a rush / hurry for time |
| 慢慢來 | màn màn lái | no rush / take it slow (very Taiwan) |
| 個性 | gèxìng | character / personality |
| 特色 | tèsè | characteristic / distinctive quality |
| 感情 | gǎnqíng | feelings / emotional connection |
| 突破 | túpò | breakthrough |
| 前進 | qiánjìn | to move forward / advance |

---

**Vocabulary note — 慢慢來 (màn màn lái):**  
This is one of the most quintessentially Taiwan phrases in daily life. It means "no rush" / "take your time." Staff say it to you. Friends say it to you. It's a small piece of cultural warmth. When someone is stressed, 慢慢來 is a way of saying: the world won't end, take it step by step. Learn it, use it, mean it.
