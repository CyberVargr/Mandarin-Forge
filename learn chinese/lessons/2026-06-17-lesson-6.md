| 10 | 正職 | zhèngzhí | full-time permanent position | 我目前在找正職的機會。Wǒ mùqián zài zhǎo zhèngzhí de jīhuì. |

---

**Taiwan vocabulary notes:**

- **應徵** — this is the Taiwan standard term for "applying for a job." Mainland Chinese often says 應聘 (yìngpìn). When you see a 職缺 you like, you 應徵 it.
- **薪水** — Taiwan. Mainland typically says 工資 or 薪資. On job postings in Taiwan you'll see 月薪 (monthly salary) and 年薪 (annual salary).
- **待遇** — includes salary, bonus, insurance (勞健保), paid leave (特休). Good word for negotiations.
- **強項** vs **優點** — 強項 (qiángxiàng) is specific to skills/professional strengths. 優點 (yōudiǎn) is more general "good points." Use 強項 in interviews.
- **擅長** — used just like "I'm skilled at ___." Structure: 我擅長 + [verb/noun]. More precise than 我很好 at.
- **自由接案** — the natural Taiwan phrase for freelancing. 案 (àn) = a case/project. 接案 = taking on projects. 自由 = free/independent.

## ⟲ BLOCK 3 — Character Writing (10 min)

Write each character 5× by hand. Understand the components before writing.

---

**1. 求 qiú — to seek / to request**  
Used in: 求職 (job hunt), 要求 (to require), 請求 (to request)  
Radical: 水 (water, simplified here as bottom strokes)  
Memory hook: 🔍 A hand reaching down into water — *seeking* something under the surface.  
Strokes: 7 | Tone: 2nd (rising)

---

**2. 職 zhí — position / duty / job**  
Used in: 求職 (job search), 正職 (full-time), 職缺 (vacancy), 職場 (workplace)  
Radical: 耳 (ear)  
Components: 耳 (ear, left) + 戠 (right — a complex component meaning 'to mark')  
Memory hook: 👂 In a workplace, you *listen* (ear) carefully to your duties — your 職.  
Strokes: 18 | Tone: 2nd (rising)  
*Note: High stroke count — focus on the left 耳 side first, then build the right.*

---

**3. 薪 xīn — salary / firewood**  
Used in: 薪水 (salary), 月薪 (monthly salary), 年薪 (annual salary)  
Radical: 艹 (grass/plant)  
Components: 艹 (plant top) + 新 (new, below)  
Memory hook: 🌿 New grass every month — something that grows and renews, like your paycheck.  
Strokes: 16 | Tone: 1st (flat)

---

**4. 應 yīng / yìng — to respond / should**  
Used in: 應徵 (to apply), 應該 (should), 回應 (to respond)  
Radical: 心 (heart)  
Memory hook: 💡 Your heart (心 at bottom) *responds* — when you feel you *should* do something, your heart says yes.  
Strokes: 17 | Tone: 1st (flat) in 應該; 4th (falling) in 應徵 → 應徵 = yìngzhēng  
*Tone note: 應 is 4th tone when it means "to apply/respond to" (應徵, 反應), 1st tone in 應該 (should).*

---

**5. 試 shì — to try / to test**  
Used in: 試用期 (probation), 面試 (interview), 考試 (exam)  
Radical: 言 (speech/word)  
Components: 言 (speech) + 式 (style/form)  
Memory hook: 🗣 You use *words* (言) in a formal *way* (式) — like answering interview questions.  
Strokes: 13 | Tone: 4th (falling)

---

## ⟲ BLOCK 4 — Grammar Pattern (10 min)

### Pattern: 我有…的經驗 / 我擅長…
**Meaning: "I have experience in…" / "I'm skilled at…"**

These two structures are your core interview sentence builders. Learn to deploy them instantly.

---

**Structure A — 我有 + [time/scope] + 的經驗:**

> 我有 [X年/X個月/[field]] 的經驗。  
> *Wǒ yǒu [X nián / X gè yuè / field] de jīngyàn.*  
> "I have [X years / months / field] of experience."

| Chinese | Pinyin | English |
|---|---|---|
| 我有十年的行銷經驗。 | Wǒ yǒu shí nián de xíngxiāo jīngyàn. | I have ten years of marketing experience. |
| 我有遊戲品牌的工作經驗。 | Wǒ yǒu yóuxì pínpái de gōngzuò jīngyàn. | I have experience working in gaming brands. |
| 我有自由接案的經驗。 | Wǒ yǒu zìyóu jiē àn de jīngyàn. | I have freelance experience. |
| 我有帶領產品上市的經驗。 | Wǒ yǒu dàilǐng chǎnpǐn shàngshì de jīngyàn. | I have experience leading product launches. |

---

**Structure B — 我擅長 + [noun / verb phrase]:**

| Chinese | Pinyin | English |
|---|---|---|
| 我擅長寫文案。 | Wǒ shàncháng xiě wénàn. | I'm skilled at writing copy. |
| 我擅長產品說明和品牌定位。 | Wǒ shàncháng chǎnpǐn shuōmíng hé pínpái dìngwèi. | I'm skilled at product messaging and brand positioning. |
| 我擅長把複雜的規格說得清楚。 | Wǒ shàncháng bǎ fùzá de guīgé shuō de qīngchǔ. | I'm skilled at explaining complex specs clearly. |
| 我擅長跨部門合作。 | Wǒ shàncháng kuà bùmén hézuò. | I'm skilled at cross-department collaboration. |

---

**Combine them (power move for interviews):**

> 我有十年的遊戲品牌行銷經驗，擅長產品文案和活動規劃。  
> *Wǒ yǒu shí nián de yóuxì pínpái xíngxiāo jīngyàn, shàncháng chǎnpǐn wénàn hé huódòng guīhuà.*  
> "I have ten years of gaming brand marketing experience, and I'm skilled at product copywriting and campaign planning."

That's a real interview opener. Learn it cold.

## ⟲ BLOCK 5 — Speaking / Dialogue (10 min)

### Scene — Mock job interview at a Taipei marketing agency

**Role A = Clark. Role B = 面試官 (miànshìguān = interviewer)**

---

> **B:** 請先做一下自我介紹。  
> *Qǐng xiān zuò yíxià zìwǒ jièshào.*  
> Please start with a brief self-introduction.

> **A:** 好的。我叫Clark，在台灣住了十多年。我之前在宏碁擔任遊戲品牌的文案主管，主要負責Predator和Nitro的行銷和產品說明。  
> *Hǎo de. Wǒ jiào Clark, zài Táiwān zhùle shí duō nián. Wǒ zhīqián zài Hóngqí dānrèn yóuxì pínpái de wénàn zhǔguǎn, zhǔyào fùzé Predator hé Nitro de xíngxiāo hé chǎnpǐn shuōmíng.*  
> Sure. My name is Clark, I've lived in Taiwan for over ten years. I previously worked at Acer as a gaming brand copy lead, mainly responsible for marketing and product messaging for Predator and Nitro.

> **B:** 你的強項是什麼？  
> *Nǐ de qiángxiàng shì shénme?*  
> What are your strengths?

> **A:** 我的強項是把複雜的技術規格轉換成清楚、有說服力的語言。我擅長文案、產品定位，還有跨部門合作。  
> *Wǒ de qiángxiàng shì bǎ fùzá de jìshù guīgé zhuǎnhuàn chéng qīngchǔ, yǒu shuōfúlì de yǔyán. Wǒ shàncháng wénàn, chǎnpǐn dìngwèi, hái yǒu kuà bùmén hézuò.*  
> My strength is converting complex technical specs into clear, persuasive language. I'm skilled at copywriting, product positioning, and cross-department collaboration.

> **B:** 你為什麼想離開現在的工作？  
> *Nǐ wèishénme xiǎng líkāi xiànzài de gōngzuò?*  
> Why do you want to leave your current job?

> **A:** 我在宏碁學到了很多，但我想找一個可以繼續成長、發揮更多創意的機會。我對品牌策略和數位行銷很感興趣。  
> *Wǒ zài Hóngqí xuédào le hěn duō, dàn wǒ xiǎng zhǎo yīgè kěyǐ jìxù chéngzhǎng, fāhuī gèng duō chuàngyì de jīhuì. Wǒ duì pínpái cèlüè hé shùwèi xíngxiāo hěn gǎn xìngqù.*  
> I've learned a lot at Acer, but I want to find an opportunity to keep growing and express more creativity. I'm very interested in brand strategy and digital marketing.

> **B:** 你的薪資期望大概是多少？  
> *Nǐ de xīnzī qīwàng dàgài shì duōshǎo?*  
> What is your salary expectation?

> **A:** 我希望可以先了解一下整體的待遇和制度，再來討論。  
> *Wǒ xīwàng kěyǐ xiān liǎojiě yíxià zhěngtǐ de dàiyù hé zhìdù, zài lái tǎolùn.*  
> I'd like to first understand the overall compensation and structure before discussing specifics.

---

**Vocabulary from this dialogue:**

| Word | Pinyin | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| 面試官 | miànshìguān | interviewer |
| 擔任 | dānrèn | to hold a position / serve as |
| 主管 | zhǔguǎn | head / manager / supervisor |
| 負責 | fùzé | to be responsible for |
| 轉換 | zhuǎnhuàn | to convert / transform |
| 說服力 | shuōfúlì | persuasiveness |
| 發揮 | fāhuī | to express / make full use of |
| 繼續成長 | jìxù chéngzhǎng | to keep growing |
| 薪資期望 | xīnzī qīwàng | salary expectation |
| 整體待遇 | zhěngtǐ dàiyù | overall compensation package |

---

**Pronunciation notes:**

- **宏碁** — Hóngqí. This is Acer's Chinese name. 2nd + 2nd tone. Good to know and say confidently.
- **擔任** dānrèn — 1st + 4th. "To serve as / hold the role of." More formal than 做 (zuò).
- **說服力** shuōfúlì — 1st + 2nd + 4th. *Persuasion power.* Very usable compound.
- **發揮** fāhuī — 1st + 1st. To give full expression to a talent or skill. Slightly elevated — good in interviews.
- The deflection on salary: **先了解…再討論** (xiān liǎojiě… zài tǎolùn) = "first understand… then discuss." Professional and natural in Taiwan.
