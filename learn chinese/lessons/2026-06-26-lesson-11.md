---

**Taiwan vocabulary notes:**

- **情境** (qíngjìng) — "situation/scenario." In interview-speak: 情境題 = behavioral/situational question. When an interviewer says 請問您有沒有遇過… ("have you ever encountered…"), they're asking a 情境題.

- **困難** (kùnnán) — "difficulty/hardship." Useful pair: 克服困難 (to overcome difficulties). Don't confuse with 困難 vs. 問題 (wèntí = problem/question). In interviews, use 困難 for human/process challenges; 問題 for technical issues.

- **克服** (kèfú) — "to overcome." 克服困難 · 克服挑戰 · 克服障礙 (overcome obstacles). This word signals resilience — use it freely in interviews.

- **當時** (dāngshí) — critical connector for storytelling. "At that time, the situation was X." 當時＋situation sets the scene for your behavioral story. It's more specific and narrative than 以前 (before) or 那時候 (at that time — more casual).

- **採取** (cǎiqǔ) — "to take (action)." 採取行動 (take action), 採取措施 (take measures). More formal than just 做 (do). Sounds deliberate in interviews: 我採取的行動是… (The action I took was…).

- **深耕** (shēngēng) — literally "to plow deep." Common in Taiwan professional language: 在這個領域深耕 = building deep expertise in this field. Signals long-term commitment and depth over breadth. Very Taiwan flavor — use it.

- **失敗** (shībài) — "failure/to fail." Interviewers ask about failure to test self-awareness. In Mandarin: 那次的失敗讓我學到了… (That failure taught me…). Turn it forward. Never just say 失敗了 (I failed) without a learning attached.

**1. 結 jié — to tie / knot / result / to conclude**  
Used in: 結果 (result), 總結 (summary/conclusion), 結束 (to end), 結合 (to combine)  
Radical: 糸/糹 (silk thread, left side)  
Components: 糹(thread) + 吉 (lucky/auspicious, right)  
Memory hook: 🧵 A thread (糹) tied to something auspicious (吉) — 結 is the moment two things come together. A knot. A result.  
Strokes: 12 | Tone: 2nd (jié, rising) — but 4th in 結實 (jiēshi: sturdy)  
*Key compounds to write: 結果、總結、結束*

---

**2. 解 jiě — to untie / to solve / to explain**  
Used in: 解決 (solve), 了解 (understand), 分解 (break down), 解釋 (explain)  
Radical: 角 (horn, left/top)  
Components: 角 (horn) + 刀 (knife) + 牛 (cow)  
Memory hook: 🐂 A knife (刀) and a horn (角) on a cow (牛) — 解 is the act of untying a knotted situation. To cut through and release.  
Strokes: 13 | Tone: 3rd (jiě, dipping)  
*Key compounds to write: 解決、了解、解釋*

---

**3. 困 kùn — trapped / difficulty / tired**  
Used in: 困難 (difficulty), 困境 (difficult situation/predicament), 困擾 (to trouble/bother)  
Radical: 囗 (enclosure, outside)  
Components: 囗 (box/enclosure) + 木 (tree inside)

*Key compounds to write: 困難、困境、困擾*

**4. 克 kè — to overcome / to control / gram**  
Used in: 克服 (overcome), 克制 (to restrain oneself), 攻克 (to capture/conquer)  
Radical: 儿 (legs/person, bottom)  
Components: 十 (ten/cross, top) + 口 (mouth, middle) + 儿 (legs, bottom)  
Memory hook: ⚔️ Standing on two legs (儿) with a cross above — 克 is the power to push through and conquer. What you do when things are 困.  
Strokes: 7 | Tone: 4th (kè, falling)  
*Key compound to write: 克服困難*

**5. 績 jī — achievement / merit / results**

Components: 糹(thread) + 責 (responsibility, right)  
Memory hook: 🏆 Thread (糹) + responsibility (責) = the results your work weaves together over time. 業績 is what you're measured by.  
Strokes: 17 | Tone: 1st (jī, flat)  
*Key compounds to write: 業績、成績、績效*

## ⟲ BLOCK 4 — Grammar Pattern (10 min)

### Pattern: STAR — Behavioral Storytelling in Mandarin  
**當時的情況是… / 我負責的是… / 我採取的行動是… / 最後的結果是…**

In behavioral interviews (行為面試), interviewers ask you to tell a story. The STAR method works in Chinese exactly as it does in English — but the sentence connectors are different. This pattern gives you a framework you can adapt to any story.

---

**The STAR frame in Mandarin:**

> **S — Situation (情境)**  
> 當時的情況是 [background].  
> *Dāngshí de qíngkuàng shì [background].*  
> "At the time, the situation was [X]."

> **T — Task (任務)**  
> 我負責的是 [what you had to do].  
> *Wǒ fùzé de shì [task].*  
> "My responsibility was [X]."

> **A — Action (行動)**  
> 我採取的行動是 [what you actually did].  
> *Wǒ cǎiqǔ de xíngdòng shì [action].*  
> "The action I took was [X]."

> **R — Result (結果)**  
> 最後的結果是 [outcome / what changed].  
> *Zuìhòu de jiéguǒ shì [result].*  
> "The final result was [X]."

---

**Optional closing — the learning:**

> 透過這次的經驗，我學到了…  
> *Tòuguò zhè cì de jīngyàn, wǒ xué dào le…*  
> "Through this experience, I learned…"

> 這讓我更了解…的重要性。  
> *Zhè ràng wǒ gèng liǎojiě… de zhòng yào xìng.*  
> "This gave me a deeper understanding of the importance of [X]."

---

**Full example — Clark's Computex 2026 story:**

> **我負責的是：** 把技術規格轉化成讓媒體和消費者都能理解的品牌語言。  
> *Wǒ fùzé de shì: Bǎ jìshù guīgé zhuǎnhuà chéng ràng méitǐ hé xiāofèizhě dōu néng lǐjiě de pǐnpái yǔyán.*

> **我採取的行動是：** 我先跟工程師開了一個快速的簡報會議，理清楚核心賣點，然後在兩天內完成了第一版的英文主要文案。  
> *Wǒ cǎiqǔ de xíngdòng shì: Wǒ xiān gēn gōngchéngshī kāi le yīgè kuàisù de jiǎnbào huìyì, lǐqīng chǔ héxīn màidiǎn, rán hòu zài liǎng tiān nèi wánchéng le dì yī bǎn de yīngwén zhǔyào wénàn.*  
> The action I took was: I first held a quick briefing with the engineers to clarify the core selling points, then produced the first version of the main English copy within two days.

> **最後的結果是：** 文案準時上線，Predator Atlas 8 的發布獲得了不少媒體的正面報導。  
> *Zuìhòu de jiéguǒ shì: Wénàn zhǔnshí shàngxiàn, Predator Atlas 8 de fābù huòdé le bù shǎo méitǐ de zhèngmiàn bàodǎo.*  
> The final result was: the copy went live on time, and the Predator Atlas 8 launch received quite a bit of positive media coverage.

> **透過這次的經驗，我學到了：** 在緊繃的時間壓力下，快速釐清優先順序是最重要的第一步。  
> *Tòuguò zhè cì de jīngyàn, wǒ xué dào le: Zài jǐnbēng de shíjiān yālì xià, kuàisù lǐqīng yōuxiān shùnxù shì zuì zhòng yào de dì yī bù.*  
> Through this experience, I learned: under tight time pressure, quickly clarifying priorities is the most important first step.

**Pronunciation notes:**

- **釐清** (lǐqīng) — 3rd+1st. Often paired with 優先順序 or 賣點. It means to sort out what's tangled. More precise than 弄清楚 (nòng qīngchǔ) in professional context.
- **對齊** (duìqí) — 4th+2nd. Taiwan tech/marketing term for "alignment meeting." 開對齊會議 (hold an alignment meeting) is extremely common in product and marketing teams in Taiwan.
- **累積** (lèijī) — 4th+1st. "To accumulate over time." 累積經驗 = build up experience. Very natural for an interview context.
- **舞台** (wǔtái) — 3rd+2nd. Literally "stage." Figuratively: arena, platform. 更大的舞台 = a bigger stage / a bigger opportunity. Sounds confident without sounding arrogant.
- **培養** (péiyǎng) — 2nd+3rd. To cultivate/develop. 培養能力 (develop ability), 培養習慣 (develop a habit). Beautiful word — implies patient, deliberate growth.
