---

**Taiwan vocabulary notes:**

- **重訓** — the everyday Taiwan word for weight training. Not 舉重 (that's competitive weightlifting). When you say 我去重訓, everyone knows what you mean.
- **組數 + 下** — 組 (zǔ) = sets, 下 (xià) = reps. "三組十下" (3 sets of 10) is how you'd say it naturally: *sān zǔ shí xià*. No filler words needed.
- **痠痛** — this is the compound for DOMS. 痠 (suān) alone means "sore / achey" (also used for sour). 痛 (tòng) alone means "pain." Together, 痠痛 = that deep post-training soreness. Don't just say 痛 — it sounds more like an injury.
- **恢復** — recovery in the full sense: physical repair, bounce-back. 恢復得好 = "recovering well." Also used for bouncing back from stress, illness, difficult periods.
- **核心** — literally "core." Used in fitness exactly as in English. Also used metaphorically — 核心理念 (core values), 核心能力 (core competency). One character to rule multiple worlds.
- **睡眠** — the full noun form of sleep. For casual speech: 睡得好嗎？(Did you sleep well?) / 昨晚睡很差 (Slept badly last night). 睡眠 is more formal/written but worth knowing.
- **狀態** — "state / form / condition." 狀態好 (good form today), 狀態差 (off-day). Extremely versatile: applies to body, work performance, mental state, even machines. One of the most reusable words in this lesson.

**1. 重 zhòng / chóng — heavy / again**  
Used in: 重訓 (weight training), 重要 (important), 重新 (again / redo)

---

**2. 恢 huī — to restore / recover**  
Used in: 恢復 (recover), 恢弘 (grand/magnificent — less common)  
Radical: 忄(heart radical — feelings/states)  
Components: 忄(heart, left) + 灰 (ash/grey, right)  
Memory hook: 🔥 From ash (灰) the heart (忄) rises — 恢復 is rising from the grey.  
Strokes: 9 | Tone: 1st (flat)

---

**3. 核 hé — core / nucleus / pit**  
Used in: 核心 (core), 核子 (nuclear), 核對 (to verify)  
Radical: 木 (wood/tree)  
Components: 木 (tree/wood, left) + 亥 (right)  
Memory hook: 🌰 The pit (核) at the center of a fruit — the hardest, densest part of the tree.  
Strokes: 10 | Tone: 2nd (rising)

---

**4. 補 bǔ — to supplement / to patch / to fill**  
Used in: 補充 (supplement/replenish), 補習 (cram school), 補眠 (catch up on sleep — very Taiwan)  
Radical: 衤(clothing radical — also used for patching)  
Memory hook: 🧵 Clothing (衤) being patched — 補 always fills in what's missing.  
Strokes: 12 | Tone: 3rd (dip)  
*Bonus Taiwan word: 補眠 (bǔ mián) = catching up on sleep. "我要去補眠" — very commonly used.*

---

**5. 態 tài — state / appearance / form**  
Used in: 狀態 (condition/form), 態度 (attitude), 形態 (form/shape)  
Radical: 心 (heart)  
Components: 能 (ability/can, top) + 心 (heart, bottom)  
Memory hook: 💡 Your *heart's ability* (能+心) is your 態 — your state of being shows in how your heart is running.  
Strokes: 14 | Tone: 4th (falling)

## ⟲ BLOCK 4 — Grammar Pattern (10 min)

### Pattern: 雖然…但是… / 雖然…還是…
**Meaning: "Even though… still…" / "Although… nevertheless…"**

This pattern expresses honest tension — the gap between how you feel and what you still do. It's one of the most natural patterns for talking about discipline, recovery, training, and resilience. It also maps perfectly onto real life.

---

**Structure:**

> 雖然 [condition/difficulty]，但是/還是 [action/result]。  
> *Suīrán [...], dànshì/háishì [...]*  
> "Even though [X], still [Y]."

**雖然…但是…** — two parts of a contrast (although X, but Y)  
**雖然…還是…** — emphasizes persistence despite difficulty (even though X, still did Y)  
In casual Taiwan speech, 雖然 is often dropped and 還是 does the work alone: 很累，還是去了。

---

**Examples:**

| Chinese | Pinyin | English |
|---|---|---|
| 雖然今天很累，但是我還是去健身了。 | Suīrán jīntiān hěn lèi, dànshì wǒ háishì qù jiànshēnle. | Even though I was tired today, I still went to the gym. |
| 雖然腿很痠痛，還是要繼續訓練。 | Suīrán tuǐ hěn suāntòng, háishì yào jìxù xùnliàn. | Even though my legs are sore, I still need to keep training. |
| 雖然睡眠不夠，但是狀態還不錯。 | Suīrán shuìmián bùgòu, dànshì zhuàngtài hái búcuò. | Even though I didn't sleep enough, my form is still not bad. |
| 雖然壓力很大，但是我每天都在練中文。 | Suīrán yālì hěn dà, dànshì wǒ měitiān dōu zài liàn Zhōngwén. | Even though the pressure is high, I practice Chinese every day. |
| 雖然狀態不好，還是去練了。 | Suīrán zhuàngtài bù hǎo, háishì qù liànle. | Even though I wasn't in good form, I still went and trained. |

---

**Casual shortcut (Taiwan speech):**

> 今天很累，還是去了。  
> *Jīntiān hěn lèi, háishì qùle.*  
> "Was tired today, still went."

Dropping 雖然 is common in spoken Mandarin. 還是 carries the "still / despite" meaning on its own. Master both versions.

## ⟲ BLOCK 5 — Speaking / Dialogue (10 min)

### Scene — Talking to a gym buddy / trainer at your local gym in Taipei

**Role A = Clark. Role B = 台灣健身房的朋友 (a Taiwanese gym friend)**

---

> **B:** 你今天來啊！昨天練完怎樣？  
> *Nǐ jīntiān lái a! Zuótiān liàn wán zěnyàng?*  
> You came today! How did you feel after training yesterday?

> **A:** 腿超痠的，但還好。今天想練上半身。  
> *Tuǐ chāo suān de, dàn hái hǎo. Jīntiān xiǎng liàn shàng bànshēn.*  
> My legs are super sore, but not bad. Today I want to train upper body.

> **B:** 那很合理。你今天幾點睡的？看起來狀態還不錯。  
> *Nà hěn hélǐ. Nǐ jīntiān jǐ diǎn shuì de? Kànqǐlái zhuàngtài hái búcuò.*  
> That makes sense. What time did you sleep last night? You look like you're in decent form.

> **A:** 大概十二點，睡了差不多七個小時。昨晚恢復得還可以。  
> *Dàgài shí'èr diǎn, shuìle chàbùduō qī gè xiǎoshí. Zuówǎn huīfù de hái kěyǐ.*  
> Around midnight, slept about seven hours. Recovery was decent last night.

> **B:** 不錯。訓練前有沒有熱身？  
> *Búcuò. Xùnliàn qián yǒu méiyǒu rèshēn?*  
> Nice. Did you warm up before training?

> **A:** 有，我都會先做一下核心和肩膀的熱身。  
> *Yǒu, wǒ dōu huì xiān zuò yíxià héxīn hé jiānbǎng de rèshēn.*  
> Yes, I always start with a little core and shoulder warm-up.

> **B:** 好習慣。訓練完記得補充蛋白質。  
> *Hǎo xíguàn. Xùnliàn wán jìde bǔchōng dànbáizhí.*  
> Good habit. Remember to replenish your protein after training.

> **A:** 知道了，我通常訓練後喝蛋白飲。  
> *Zhīdào le, wǒ tōngcháng xùnliàn hòu hē dànbái yǐn.*  
> I know — I usually have a protein drink after training.

---

**Vocabulary from this dialogue:**

| Word | Pinyin | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| 超痠 | chāo suān | super sore (超 = super/so, Taiwan intensifier) |
| 上半身 | shàng bànshēn | upper body |
| 合理 | hélǐ | makes sense / reasonable |
| 差不多 | chàbuduō | approximately / almost |
| 肩膀 | jiānbǎng | shoulder(s) |
| 好習慣 | hǎo xíguàn | good habit |
| 蛋白飲 | dànbái yǐn | protein drink |
| 知道了 | zhīdào le | I know / got it (closing acknowledgment) |

---

**Pronunciation notes:**

- **超** (chāo) — 1st tone, flat. Taiwan's favorite intensifier. 超好 (super good), 超累 (super tired), 超痠 (super sore). Learn it as a prefix.
- **差不多** dàbuduō — 4th+0+1st. "About / roughly." Very common. The 不 goes unstressed/neutral in fast speech.
- **合理** hélǐ — 2nd+3rd. "Makes sense / reasonable / logical." Natural and versatile.
- **知道了** zhīdào le — 1st+4th+neutral. The final 了 signals completion of understanding. Friendly, casual acknowledgment. Don't just say 知道 — add 了 for natural flow.
- Watch your tones: 恢復 (huī 1st · fù 4th), 熱身 (rè 4th · shēn 1st), 補充 (bǔ 3rd · chōng 1st)

## ⟲ BLOCK 6 — Self-Expression (10 min)

### Real sentences for your body and your training life

These are all things you can actually say. They connect your fitness life to natural Chinese.

---

**1. 我平常一週練三到四次，主要是重訓。**  
*Wǒ píngcháng yì zhōu liàn sān dào sì cì, zhǔyào shì zhòngxùn.*  
"I usually train three to four times a week — mainly weight training."

- Casual: 我大概一週練個三四次，都是重訓。  
  *(I train about 3–4 times a week, all lifting.)*
- You can swap in: 拳擊訓練 (boxing training), 有氧運動 (cardio), 格鬥 (combat/martial arts)

---

**2. 我最近睡眠品質不太好，恢復也慢了一點。**  
*Wǒ zuìjìn shuìmián pǐnzhì bú tài hǎo, huīfù yě màn le yīdiǎn.*  
"My sleep quality hasn't been great recently, and my recovery has slowed a bit too."

- 睡眠品質 (shuìmián pǐnzhì) = sleep quality — learn this as a fixed phrase.
- Add: 可能是壓力的關係。*(kěnéng shì yālì de guānxi — probably stress-related)*

---

**3. 雖然最近壓力很大，但是我還是堅持練習。**  
*Suīrán zuìjìn yālì hěn dà, dànshì wǒ háishì jiānchí liànxí.*  
"Even though the pressure lately has been high, I'm still sticking with training."

- 堅持 (jiānchí) — to persist / to stick with something. This word is important for your life right now.  
  堅持下去 = keep going / don't stop. Burn it in.

---

**4. 訓練讓我的思路比較清楚，壓力也小一點。**  
*Xùnliàn ràng wǒ de sīlù bǐjiào qīngchǔ, yālì yě xiǎo yīdiǎn.*  
"Training helps my thinking feel clearer, and the pressure eases a bit too."

- 思路 (sīlù) — your train of thought / mental flow. Slightly elevated but natural in Taiwan speech.
- 讓 (ràng) — "to let / to make / to cause." 訓練讓我… = "Training makes me…" — extremely useful structure.

---

**5. 我覺得恢復跟睡眠一樣重要，不只是訓練本身。**  
*Wǒ juéde huīfù gēn shuìmián yīyàng zhòngyào, bù zhǐshì xùnliàn běnshēn.*  
"I think recovery and sleep are just as important — not just the training itself."

- 一樣重要 (yīyàng zhòngyào) — "equally important." Clean comparative structure.
- 不只是 (bù zhǐshì) — "not just / not only." Great for nuanced statements.
